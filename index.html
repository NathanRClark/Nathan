About Me

View Resume

I am a tenor and classical vocalist studying Vocal Performance at Baylor University, passionate about bringing classical music to life through expressive performances and meaningful connections with audiences.

View Resume

At Baylor, I am a proud member of Baylor A Cappella, Chamber Singers, and Baylor Opera. With Baylor Opera, I performed in our recent production of Notes on Viardot as an ensemble member and one of the Bandits, and I will be part of our upcoming production of L'Orfeo as an ensemble member and part of the Pastori. Earlier this year, I performed with Baylor Opera at the National Opera Conference in Boston and presented my poster, representing the program on a national stage.

I study under Dr. Mark Diamond and also receive coachings from Dr. Jeffrey Peterson, refining my technique and expanding my vocal artistry.

Beyond the university, I serve in the music ministry at St. Louis Catholic Church, where I continue to combine my love for music with my faith. One of my most transformative experiences was touring with the St. Paul Choral Scholars across Austria, performing in historic cathedrals including Salzburg Cathedral and St. Stephen's Cathedral in Vienna, which deepened my appreciation for sacred music and its rich cultural heritage.

When I'm not performing or practicing, you'll find me pursuing my passion for physical fitness, whether that's at the gym, engaging in sports, or challenging myself to reach new personal bests. I believe in the strong connection between physical wellness and vocal performance.
